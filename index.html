A Little About Myself

Shortly after graduating high school, I went to Penn State Wilkes-Barre to study Information Science & Technology, with the sole purpose being, "If I'm gonna try to go into an industry where I'll be on a computer most of the time, I might as well get a degree understanding them". Turns out, writing code is both calming and fun (so long as said code has no errors. Finding errors without the aid of a program is a nightmare). Since graduating from Penn State with my Bachelor of Science degree in IST, I've begun the process of gaining work experience. From my brief internship as an IT Representative at a restaurant, to working for TDCJ as a Java prgrammer, I figure I've been doing decently well. As of writing this, I have intermediate knowledge of HTML and CSS, as well as a basic understanding of Java (primarily for android application development).

As I mentioned in the opening, I want to study and design video games, hopefully in the near future. This determination started back on my 9th birthday, when I received my first gaming console and video game (original Xbox and Start Wars: Knights of the Old Republic). I must have put a month's worth of time playing it, exploring multiple paths, story lines, moral choices, play styles, equipment combinations, team balance, etc. When I done all that I could do at the time, I knew what I wanted to do when I grew up: to design video games. It wasn't until high school where I realized what facet of game design I wanted to do, that being animation (I figured that If I studied animation and couldn't make it in the game industry, I could still use animation as another career path). My goal is to go back to school to study game design and animation (possibly art if that I need too), and eventually earn a degree in that field

When I'm not working, studying, or making my plans for world domination, I'm typically at home hanging out. That normally includes, but certainly not limited to: playing and understanding video games (mostly on the PS4. Love it or hate it, it has some of the best exclusive games...and that's coming from someone supported Microsoft/Xbox for over 7 years), making and assembling knives (I plan on making stock removal knives), creating car decals through media from the internet (specializing in video games, anime, and internet memes), and spoiling my my cat CC. I'm also planning on setting some time aside to dig into my list of subjects that I've always wanted to learn.
